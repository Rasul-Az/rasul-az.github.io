Research Interests

    I am a passionate researcher in the fields of Non-Destructive Testing (NDT), Microwave Photonics, Antenna Technology, Fiber Optics, and Algebra. My research focus spans a variety of topics, including NDT and the design of low-frequency sensors, sub-6 GHz antennas, and phased array antennas for 5G applications. Additionally, I explore THz applications, focusing on graphene-based antenna design and the development of microwave photonic techniques for advanced wireless communications. Furthermore, I have expertise in linear algebra, including:
  • Direct and iterative methods for solving systems of linear equations and analyzing their convergence.
  • Examining the impact of disturbances in the data of a system on its solutions.
  • Approximate calculation of eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
  • Applications of Singular Value Decomposition (SVD) in image processing, data mining, and compression.
